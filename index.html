Phase 1: Initial Healing and Protection (0-3 Weeks)

Protect the tendon, reduce swelling, and prevent stiffness. Gentle exercises to start your recovery journey.

  • Non-Weight Bearing Range of Motion (ROM): Gentle ankle pumps (up and down) while keeping the leg elevated.
  • Isometric Contractions: pressing the foot gently against a resistance (like a wall) without moving the ankle.
  • Toe Flexor Exercises: Toe curls with a resistant band or a towel to maintain muscle activity within the calfs

Phase 2: Early Mobility and Strengthening (3-6 Weeks)

Regain mobility and begin gentle strengthening exercises to rebuild strength and flexibility.

  • Active Range of Motion (AROM): Gentle circles and alphabet with the affected foot.
  • Calf Stretching (Non-Weight Bearing): Using a towel or resistant band gently stretch the calf without stressing the Achilles.
  • Towel Scrunches and Marble Pick-ups: Place a towel on the floor with the affected foot scrunch the towel towrds you / place a marble on the floor and gently pick it up with the affected foot.

Phase 3: Progessive Strengthening with simple Plyometrics (6-12 Weeks)

Improve strength, balance and coordination.

  • Heel Raises (Double Leg): Start with both legs and progress to single-leg heel raises as strength improves.
  • Resistant Exercises: Resistance band exercises for plantar flexion, dorsiflexion, inversion, and eversion.
  • Balance Exercises: Standing on one leg and progressing to dynamic balance activities like standing on an uneven surface.
  • Simple Plyometrics: Squats (10 reps 1 set) Lunges (10 reps 1 set)

Phase 4: More Advanced Strengthening and Plyometrics (12-16 Weeks)

Further improving strength, balance and advanced motion.

  • Balance Drills: Single leg squats, step ups and lateral step ups (15 reps 3 sets)
  • Wall sits: Stand in front of a wall with your feet shoulder-width apart. Press your back against the wall and lower yourself until your knees and hips are both at a right angle with your thighs parallel to the floor. (30 secodns 3 sets)

Phase 5: Advanced Strengthening and Plyometrics (16-24 Weeks)

Build advanced strength and prepare for a return to sports.

  • Single-Leg Heel Raises: Focusing on eccentric control (slow lowering)
  • Plyometric Drills: Light jumping and hopping exercises, progressing to more dynamic activities.
  • Agility Exercises: Ladder drills, side shuffles, and cutting movements

Phase 6: Return to Sport/Activity (24+)

Achieve full function and confidence for sports or daily activities.

  • Sport-Specific Drills: Start with non-contact drills and progress to contact drills as tolerated.
  • Functional Movement Patterns: Mimicking sport-specific movements to build confidence and strength.
  • Dynamic Agility Drills: Combining speed, agility, and power movements
